 # Lesson II: Related Fields (Summary)

## 1. Artificial Intelligence (AI)

* AI is a **subfield of Computer Science**.
* It focuses on creating systems that can perform tasks requiring human intelligence such as learning, reasoning, perception, and decision-making.

---

## 2. Machine Learning (ML)

* **Machine Learning is a subfield of AI.**
* ML systems improve their performance automatically by learning from data and experience instead of being explicitly programmed.

**Definition:**

> Systems that improve their performance in a given task with more experience or data.

**Examples**

* Spam detection
* Recommendation systems (Netflix, YouTube)
* Fraud detection

---

## 3. Deep Learning (DL)

* **Deep Learning is a subfield of Machine Learning.**
* Uses complex neural networks with many layers ("deep" refers to the depth of the model).
* Became practical because of increased computing power.

**Examples**

* ChatGPT
* Face recognition
* Speech recognition
* Image classification

Relationship:

```
Computer Science
    └── AI
         └── Machine Learning
                └── Deep Learning
```

---

## 4. Data Science

* Data Science is a broad field combining:

  * Statistics
  * Machine Learning
  * Algorithms
  * Databases
  * Web Applications
  * Domain Knowledge (Business, Healthcare, Finance, etc.)

Purpose:

* Extract insights and value from data.

Data Science often uses AI but is not entirely AI.

---

## 5. Robotics

Robotics involves building machines that can sense, think, and act in the real world.

It combines many AI areas:

* Computer Vision
* Speech Recognition
* Natural Language Processing
* Planning
* Reasoning
* Machine Learning

Most modern robots rely heavily on Machine Learning.

Examples:

* Self-driving cars
* Warehouse robots
* Drones
* Industrial robots

---

## 6. What is a Robot?

A robot consists of:

* **Sensors** → Observe the environment
* **Actuators** → Perform actions
* **Program** → Controls behavior

Examples:

* Autonomous cars
* Factory robots
* Cleaning robots

A chatbot is **not** considered a robot because it has no physical sensors or actuators.

* **AI** is a branch of Computer Science.

# Lession III - Philosophy of AI**

The philosophy of AI explores whether machines can truly be intelligent or conscious, or if they only appear intelligent through their behavior.

### **1. The Turing Test**

* Proposed by **Alan Turing** to measure machine intelligence.
* A computer passes the test if a human cannot distinguish it from another human through text conversation.
* It focuses on **behavior**, not whether the machine actually understands or thinks.

### **2. Criticism of the Turing Test**

* Passing the test does not necessarily mean the machine is intelligent.
* Chatbots like **Eugene Goostman** fooled some judges by avoiding questions, making jokes, and acting like a teenager rather than demonstrating real understanding.
* The test measures **human-like behavior**, not true intelligence.

### **3. The Chinese Room Argument**

* Proposed by **John Searle**.
* A person who doesn't know Chinese follows a rulebook to respond correctly to Chinese messages.
* Although the responses appear intelligent, the person does **not understand Chinese**.
* Similarly, AI may process information correctly without genuine understanding or consciousness.

### **4. AI in Practice**

* AI systems like **self-driving cars** perform intelligent tasks but do not truly "understand" their environment like humans.
* Most AI researchers focus on building useful systems rather than solving philosophical questions about consciousness.

### **5. General AI vs. Narrow AI**

* **Narrow AI:** Designed for specific tasks (e.g., chatbots, recommendation systems, self-driving cars). This is the AI we use today.
* **General AI (AGI):** A hypothetical AI capable of performing any intellectual task a human can do. It does not yet exist.

### **6. Strong AI vs. Weak AI**

* **Strong AI:** A machine with genuine intelligence and consciousness.
* **Weak AI:** AI that behaves intelligently but does not possess real understanding or consciousness. All current AI systems are considered weak AI.

### **Key Takeaway**

Current AI is **Narrow AI** and **Weak AI**—it can perform specific tasks effectively but does not truly think, understand, or possess consciousness like humans. The philosophical debate continues, but AI research mainly focuses on developing practical and useful systems.
